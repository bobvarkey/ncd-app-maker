How to read the panel

PatternTypical interpretation
High TSH + low FT4Primary hypothyroidism; anti-TPO positivity supports Hashimoto pattern.
High TSH + normal FT4Subclinical hypothyroidism.
Low TSH + high FT4 or T3Overt thyrotoxicosis/hyperthyroidism.
Low TSH + normal FT4/FT3Subclinical hyperthyroidism, early Graves, thyroiditis, or over-replacement.
Low/normal TSH + low FT4Central hypothyroidism or severe non-thyroidal illness; do not rely on TSH alone.

Assessment

Common thyroid tests explained

TSH

Best screening test in most ambulatory adults. High TSH usually points to primary hypothyroidism, while low or suppressed TSH usually points to thyrotoxicosis.

Free T4

Confirms hormone deficiency or excess.A low FT4 with high TSH indicates overt primary hypothyroidism; a high FT4 with low TSH indicates overt hyperthyroidism.

T3

Most useful when hyperthyroidism is suspected and FT4 is normal or only slightly high, because some patients have T3-predominant thyrotoxicosis.

Anti-TPO / TgAb

Support autoimmune hypothyroidism, especially Hashimoto disease. Antibody positivity also increases the likelihood that subclinical hypothyroidism will progress.

TRAb / TSI

Supports Graves disease in a thyrotoxic patient and helps distinguish Graves from destructive thyroiditis when uptake scanning is not available or not suitable.

Other tests

Ultrasound is for nodules or structural disease, not routine biochemical diagnosis. Calcitonin and thyroglobulin are niche tests with specific oncology indications.
